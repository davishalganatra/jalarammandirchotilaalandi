Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about visiting Jalaram Mandir

The temple is open daily from 5:00 AM to 9:00 PM. During special festivals and occasions, the temple may remain open for extended hours. The aarti timings are as follows:

  • Mangla Aarti: 5:30 AM
  • Shringar Aarti: 8:00 AM
  • Sandhya Aarti: 6:30 PM
  • Shayan Aarti: 8:30 PM

Jalaram Mandir is located in Chotila, Gujarat, approximately 50 km from Rajkot. You can reach the temple by:

  • By Road: Well-connected by state highways. Regular buses and taxis available from Rajkot, Ahmedabad, and other major cities.
  • By Train: The nearest railway station is Rajkot (50 km away). From there, you can take a bus or taxi to Chotila.
  • By Air: The nearest airport is Rajkot Airport (55 km away).

Yes, you can book special poojas and sevas in advance through our online portal or by contacting the temple office. Some of the sevas that can be booked include:

  • Abhishekam
  • Satyanarayan Katha
  • Special Aarti
  • Annadanam (Food offering)

For more information and booking, please visit our Seva Booking page.

Yes, Jalaram Mandir provides the following facilities for devotees:

  • Atithi Gruh (Guest House): Comfortable accommodation is available for devotees. Rooms can be booked in advance through our Guest House Booking system.
  • Sadavrat (Free Meal Service): The temple provides free meals to all visitors twice daily (lunch and dinner). The dining hall is open from 11:30 AM to 2:30 PM and 7:00 PM to 9:00 PM.

Donations can be made through various methods:

  • Online: Visit our Donation Portal to make a secure online donation.
  • Bank Transfer: You can transfer funds directly to the temple's bank account (details available on request).
  • In-Person: Donation boxes are available throughout the temple complex.
  • Cheque/DD: Payable to "Jalaram Mandir Trust" can be sent to the temple office.

All donations are eligible for tax exemption under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act.

About Jalaram Mandir

Jalaram Mandir in Chotila is a sacred shrine dedicated to Jalaram Bapa, a revered saint known for his selfless service and devotion.
